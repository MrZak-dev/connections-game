What is Connections NYT?

Connections NYT is a daily word association puzzle created by The New York Times. It challenges players to find the hidden links between words, making it a perfect mental workout for fans of word games and brain teasers. Each day presents a fresh grid and a new challenge, keeping you on your toes.

The Core Gameplay

The game presents you with 16 words, and your job is to sort them into four distinct groups of four. A common theme connects each group. You have four chances to make a mistake before the game is over. Correctly identifying a group reveals its category and color-codes it, helping you narrow down the remaining options.

Connections Today’s Difficulty Explained

Each puzzle has an official 'Daily Difficulty' score, which is an average rating based on how a group of human solvers performed during pre-release testing. This gives you a general idea of how challenging the puzzle might be. However, remember that puzzle-solving is personal, and your own experience may vary from this official rating!

Understanding The NYT Connections Colors

When you correctly identify a group, it will be assigned a color that indicates its difficulty level. This system helps you understand the puzzle's logic.

  • Yellow: The most straightforward and easiest category to find.
  • Green: A moderate challenge, but still reasonably easy to guess.
  • Blue: Often involves more specialized trivia, cultural references, or unfamiliar concepts.
  • Purple: The most cryptic and complex category, usually involving clever wordplay or puns.

At the end of the game, you can share your colorful results grid with friends!

Pro Tips to Master Connections NYT

Want to improve your solving streak? Keep these simple strategies in mind.

Get to Know Your Trivia

The game's editor has noted that the mid-tier categories (Blue and Green) often rely on trivia or specific synonyms. Brushing up on your general knowledge about everything from car models to movie directors can give you a huge advantage and help you spot these trickier connections.

Get Your Guesses Out of The Way

If you spot a group that you're almost certain is correct, it's often a good strategy to lock it in early. Every correct guess removes four words from the board, reducing the remaining options and making it much easier to determine the final categories.

Don't Fall for Obvious Traps

The puzzle designers love to include red herrings! You might see four words that seem to fit an obvious category (like SPONGE, BOB, SQUARE, and PANTS), but they are actually part of four different, more subtle groups. If a connection seems too easy, take a second look and consider other possibilities.

What is Connections NYT by The New York Times?

Connections NYT is a word association game in which players must find common themes among 16 words and sort them into four groups of four. It's a daily puzzle that tests your logic and general knowledge.

How to Solve The NYT Connections Puzzle?

To solve it, carefully examine all 16 words and look for potential links. Group words that share a common category, select your four-word guess, and submit it. Repeat until you have solved all four groups.

What are the rules of the Connections Game?

The main rules are to sort the 16 words into four groups of four based on a shared property. You have four mistakes allowed. A correct guess reveals a group, while an incorrect guess costs you a life.

What is the Objective of the Connections Game?

The objective is to correctly identify all four hidden categories and assign the correct four words to each one before you make four mistakes.

How Many Mistakes Can I Make In The Connections Game?

You can make up to four mistakes. The game ends after the fourth incorrect guess.
